Overview:

Mangueshi Temple is a significant Hindu temple located in Priol, Ponda Taluk in Goa. It is the only temple in Goa where Lord Shiva is known as Lord Manguesh and this name not used for Lord Shiva anywhere else in India. Mangueshi Lord is one of the local deity's of Goan and Maharashtrians.

Mangueshi temple is one of the most popular temples in the state and an important pilgrimage and tourist destination in Goa because of its calm atmosphere, beautiful architecture, and historical significance. On the other hand, the construction of this temple combines elements of Portuguese design with pure Goan and Hindu architecture. This temple is very simple in its architecture and is recognized by the standout white structure and the Nandi bull that sits at the front of it.

When to visit

High Season

When: June and August

What to Expect:

  • Accommodation rates are at an all-time high, and any form of discount is unlikely to be obtained.
  • Winter also offers a little more rain, as it falls during the Northeast Monsoon season, but you won't notice much of a difference in the weather regardless of when you visit.
  • The Great Singapore Sale, which takes place in June and July, attracts even more visitors, with hotels and other lodgings packed.

Low Season

When: August through October, except mid-September

What to Expect:

  • The Southwest Monsoon arrives about this time, but it is less intense than the Northeast Monsoon.
  • Temperatures are also a little higher than they are for the rest of the year but that doesn’t impact the influx of tourists.
  • This is probably the best time to try to get a better deal on a hotel room and flight.

Shoulder Season

When:Mid-January through May

What to Expect:

  • Shoulder season months are great because there isn't much rain and they are in the middle of winters and summers.
  • April is a wonderful month to visit Singapore because there are no public holidays and it does not fall during the rainy season.
  • October is another shoulder month with few crowds but plenty of fascinating events and festivals to enjoy for visitors.

When is the Best Time to Visit Mangueshi Temple?

Mangueshi Temple can be visited any time of the year and will never fail to offer the delight. But there are times when you should go because the weather is great or a particular festival is happening.

Winter Season:

November to February

Average Temperatures: 19°C - 32°C

It is the most peak season to visit to Mangueshi Temple. The days are generally cold but the weather is good and overnight temperatures fall down a bit. Opening up the shrine for longer hours because of the festival season, has made it easy to discover the temple and its vicinity.

Summer Season

March to May

Average Temperature: 24°C - 37°C

Summer may be hot and humid, while the mornings and evenings might still be bearable, often making a midday visit unbearable. In summer, you can visit this place in early morning or late in the afternoon after 5.00 pm.

Monsoon Season

June to September

Average Temperatures: 23°C - 30°C

Goa experiences heavy rainfall during the monsoon season. The lush greenery and scenic beauty are at their peak, heavy rains can sometimes disrupt travel plans and those who enjoy fewer crowds, and this might be a great time to visit.

Plan your visit to Mangueshi Temple by Bus

It is fairly easy to reach Mangueshi Temple by bus as the temple can be accessed very easily by frequent buses being operated in Goa.

  • Panaji Kadamba Bus Stand is a main bus stand in Goa.
  • Take a bus to Ponda, which is the nearest big town to Mangueshi Temple, buses to Ponda are numerous and you should not have too much of a problem finding them.
  • Distance between Panaji to Ponda is 28 kms and it takes 45- 1hr to reach Ponda from Panaji.
  • Once at the Ponda bus stand take a local bus to Priol or Mangeshi.
  • Ponda to Mangeshi Temple is nearly 8 kms by Road; Journey time will take around 20-30 minutes.

  • Madgaon Kadamba Bus Stand is a one of the main bus stand in South Goa.
  • Take a bus to Ponda, which is the nearest big town to Mangueshi Temple, buses to Ponda are numerous and you should not have too much of a problem finding them.
  • Distance between Madgaon to Ponda is 20 kms and it takes 45 minutes - 1hr to reach Ponda from Madgaon .
  • Once at the Ponda bus stand take a local bus to Priol or Mangeshi
  • Ponda to Mangeshi Temple is nearly 8 kms by Road; Journey time will take around 20-30 minutes.

  • Check the buses: Local bus timings change so make sure you know the latest times of operation. It is advised that you confirm the times in advance
  • Ask For Help: Enquire the bus conductors or local people for the route; which bus you should take. Most of the people in Goa are helpful.
  • Language: English and Hindi are widely spoken, so communication should not be an issue when asking for directions or help.
  • Small Change: Carry in small currency denominations as this will come handy while travelling by bus
  • Travel Time: If you are catching a bus, factor in more time as you may have to wait.

Visitor Tips

  • Photography: Photography might be restricted in certain areas of the temple. Always check for signage or ask temple authorities for guidance.
  • Behaviour: Respectful behaviour, avoids littering, keep noise levels low, and avoid any actions that might be considered disrespectful within the temple premises.
  • Dress Code: Visitors must wear elegantly Men wear full shirts and pants or traditional wear women are supposed to come in sarees and salwar kameez or similar mild dress
  • Footwear: Shoes/slippers footwear’s any, must be removed before you enter the temple.

Visitor info

  • Opening Hours: The temple is generally open from early morning (around 6:00 AM) to late evening (around 10:00 PM). This will depend on the specific hours of different festivals.
  • Days: Go on the weekdays it would be nicer with less people vs going on the weekends.
  • Early Morning/ Late Evening Visits: Try to visit during early morning or late evening to get a peaceful and soothing experience with fewer visitors and pleasant weather.
  • Donations: Donations are also accepted and optional by pay for the upkeep of the temple.
